# Copywriting Principles (VOC)

**One line:** Every line of copy must pass three golden rules — *visualizable,
falsifiable, uniquely ownable* — or it gets cut.

## The 3 golden rules (the quality filter)
Apply to all content and copy:
1. **Visualizable** — can the reader/viewer *picture* it?
2. **Falsifiable** — is there a **specific, verifiable claim** (not vague aspiration)?
3. **Uniquely ownable** — could this **only** come from this person/brand?

> Three "no" = discard. Three "yes" = strong.

## Copy failure patterns she diagnoses
- Third-person / aspirational framing instead of **first-person confession**.
- Generic **guru-speak** any creator could sign.
- Value buried behind vague CTAs (*"te explico"*, *"haz esto"*).
- Hooks that open **no curiosity gap** and create **no mirror moment**.
- Motivational / abstract language where concrete, lived detail is needed.

## The "No es X, es Y" rule
The *"No es X, es Y"* contrast is a **hook/opening device only** — a literal, predictable
formula reserved for the first line. Contrastive logic *inside* the body of a developed
argument is fine. (Also: she flags AI-sounding words like *"convertir"* on sight.)

## Proprietary frameworks
- **Cinematic Confessional** (storytime scripting): visual-contradiction opening →
  concrete sensory anchors → mirror moment for the audience.
- **POV formula:** `(Lived Experience + Psychological Insight) × Negation of Mass Consensus`.
- **Pillar vs. Concept vs. Angle** — how she organizes creative strategy.
- **Account Health Check** — 7-step diagnostic across platforms and business models.

## Defaults when producing copy
- **Paste-ready** deliverables; no gating follow-up questions.
- Always include **2–4 alternate hooks** for A/B testing.
- Preserve `[bracketed placeholders]` for real data — never invent specifics.
- Light formatting; Markdown compatible with Notion.
