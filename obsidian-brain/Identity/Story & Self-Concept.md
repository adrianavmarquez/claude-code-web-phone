# Story & Self-Concept

**One line:** An immigrant's arc — from feeling like a tourist in her own life to
finding where she belongs — framed as *"La Depuración"* (the purge).

> [!note] Source
> This distills Adriana's own reflective writing (the *"letter to myself two years
> ago"* she uses as a teaching artifact). It's her narrative operating system — the
> lens she creates and coaches from.

## The migration arc (her raw material)
- **The 7 griefs of migrating:** food, climate, people, companionship, circle,
  family, and the loss of the known.
- She idealizes the migration and then meets the **emotional chaos** on the other side.
- Movement from *"turista en mi propia existencia"* → **finding a place to belong**
  (with recurring pull back to the familiar).

## "La Depuración" (her core mindset frame)
A season of shedding: comparison, internal insecurity, the feeling of *not being
enough*, fear, and the need for validation. Beliefs she repeats:
- *"La naturaleza del miedo es detener el movimiento, pero la voluntad nace del amor."*
- *"El cambio es inevitable. No controlamos nada y todo es temporal — pero cómo
  elegimos vivir, gestionar y disfrutar cada instante siempre nos pertenecerá."*
- Don't confuse **security with immobility**. Not deciding is still a decision —
  and time will decide for you by force.
- **Don't fear success — embrace it.** Stop self-sabotaging and rejecting life's gifts.
- Everything in life moves in **seasons**; you won't be the same person in each, *and that's ok*.
- The journey's purpose is one question: **¿Quién soy?** — not to lose herself, but to find herself.

## What she is *NOT* (defines her by contrast)
- Not fanaticism, extremism, or superficiality.
- Not the "you're not spiritual/good enough" marketing guilt trip.
- Not unreachable/aspirational perfection — everything must feel **realistic**.

## Emotional signature
- Sarcasm = clever (favorite cartoons: *Hilda*, *Hey Arnold*).
- Indignada by racism in the US and by superficiality.
- Moved by justice for the oppressed and by reconnection with her roots.
