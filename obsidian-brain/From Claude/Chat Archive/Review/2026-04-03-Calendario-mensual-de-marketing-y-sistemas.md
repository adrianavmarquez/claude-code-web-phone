## 🤖 Claude · 2026-04-03 19:24
Here are the best places to see how people search on Google, from free to paid:

---

**Google's own free tools**

**Google Trends** — [trends.google.com](https://trends.google.com) — This is the main one. It analyzes aggregated and anonymized search data from Google and YouTube to reveal insights into search behaviors over time. You can see how interest in a keyword grows or drops, compare up to 5 terms at once, and filter by country, city, time range, and even by platform (Web, YouTube, Shopping, News). Related Topics and Related Queries sections give you content ideas and long-tail keywords.

**Google Search Console** — [search.google.com/search-console](https://search.google.com/search-console) — Shows you exactly what queries people use to find *your* website: clicks, impressions, CTR, and average position. Essential for understanding what's already working.

**Google Keyword Planner** — Inside Google Ads. Shows monthly search volume estimates, competition level, and CPC for any keyword. Requires a Google Ads account (free to access even without running ads).

**People Also Ask & Autocomplete** — Just search anything on Google. The autocomplete suggestions and "People Also Ask" boxes show real patterns in how people phrase their searches.

---

**What changed in 2025 to pay attention to**

"Tell me about…" queries surged 70% year-over-year, and "How do I…" searches hit an all-time high with a 25% increase — showing people now search in a more conversational, question-based way rather than fragmented keywords. This matters a lot for how you write content and pick keywords.

Google Trends now groups related searches into semantic clusters powered by AI, helping you uncover themes beyond just individual keywords.

---

**Paid tools that go deeper**

| Tool | What it adds |
|---|---|
| **SEMrush** | Full keyword database, competitor traffic, intent analysis |
| **Ahrefs** | Backlinks + keyword difficulty + content gap analysis |
| **SpyFu** | See what keywords competitors buy on Google Ads |
| **Similarweb** | Audience behavior, traffic sources, engagement metrics |

---

For a marketing team, the most practical combo is: **Google Trends** (what the world is searching) + **Search Console** (how your site performs) + **GA4** (what users do after they click). Want me to build a dashboard that pulls all three together?
