# Three rules for testing copywriting quality

> **Conversation Overview**

The person shared a framework they use to evaluate copywriting quality and asked Claude to save it to memory. The framework consists of three diagnostic questions to apply to any written content: "Can I visualize it?", "Can I falsify it?", and "Can nobody else say it?" The scoring logic is binary and unforgiving — three "no" answers signals the copy is weak, while three "yes" answers signals strong, distinctive work. Claude saved this to memory and briefly reflected on the framework's logic, noting its all-or-nothing nature as a feature rather than a flaw.

This framework should be applied when helping this person evaluate, write, or critique copy going forward.

## 🤖 Claude · 2026-04-19 23:11
Guardado. Esas tres preguntas van a vivir en cada pieza que trabajemos juntas.

Y son brutales precisamente porque no tienen término medio. O pasan las tres o no pasan ninguna. No hay "casi específico" ni "medio falsificable." El copy o tiene dientes o no los tiene.
