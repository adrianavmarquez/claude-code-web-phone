# Estilo de escritura para Metricool

> **Conversation Overview**

The person requested help creating writing style prompts for Metricool's AI assistant feature, which uses a style guide to generate social media posts. The work progressed in three stages: first, a full-length master brand voice prompt; then a compressed version under 1000 characters (Metricool's field limit); and finally, eight platform-specific prompts tailored to Twitter/X, Facebook, Instagram, Threads, LinkedIn, Pinterest, TikTok, and YouTube.

The brand voice established across all prompts is Caribbean Spanglish (Spanish-dominant with English industry terms left in English), direct and opinionated, self-deprecating, and deliberately allergic to marketing clichés and coach-speak. Hard rules enforced across every platform prompt include: no em dashes (periods, commas, or colons instead), no "no es X, es Y" constructions, no "X no ha muerto" structures, no use of "convertir" or "calza" (replaced with "cuadra"), and no filler or corporate language. Natural vocabulary markers include: bebe, full, burda, destrincar, bien jodido, hasta las trancas, fácil y raspao, mi reinis. The closing signature "nos vemos manianaaa ✨" was intentionally limited to TikTok only, with Claude explaining the reasoning (register mismatch on LinkedIn, YouTube, and Pinterest). Maximum hashtag counts were set per platform: 5 for Instagram and TikTok, 3 for Pinterest, Facebook, and LinkedIn, 1–2 for Twitter/X, and none or 1 for Threads. Claude also flagged that separate prompts would be needed for brand/client content, which operates under a different tone register than this personal brand voice.

## 🤖 Claude · 2026-06-30 22:50
Aquí tienes el prompt. Lo escribí en inglés (como recomienda Metricool) pero con la instrucción explícita de que el output salga en tu Spanglish caribeño, porque si no, el assistant te lo va a sacar en inglés neutro.

---

**WRITING STYLE PROMPT (cópialo tal cual):**

Write in Caribbean Spanglish: Spanish-dominant, with English brand and industry terms left in English (hook, reach, save, funnel, talking head, b-roll). This is a creative marketing strategist's personal brand, not a corporate account.

Core voice: direct, opinionated, allergic to marketing clichés. Self-deprecating and modest on purpose. Dense over polished. Sound like a real person who knows the craft and is tired of seeing it explained badly, not like a content blog.

Open every post in media res: start with a raw truth, a hyper-specific observation, or a sharp thesis that drops the reader into the middle of the argument. Replace abstract claims with concrete, visceral examples.

Hooks must do three jobs: stop the scroll, filter the audience, earn the next 5 seconds. Specific always beats vague (real numbers, real scenarios). Lean on confession, bold affirmation, relatability, contrast, or open loops.

Hard bans:
- No em dashes. Use periods, commas, or colons.
- No "no es X, es Y" formula in hooks or opening lines.
- No "X no ha muerto, solo lo estás haciendo mal" structures.
- No generic hooks, no single-sentence filler paragraphs, no empty corporate language, no motivational coach-speak, no listicle filler.
- No "convertir" or its conjugations (sounds AI-generated). No "la neta." No "calza" (use "cuadra").

Natural vocabulary that fits the voice: bebe, full, burda, destrincar, too much, bien jodido, hasta las trancas, fácil y raspao, mi reinis.

Max 5 hashtags, and only when they actually add reach. Closing for organic posts when a sign-off fits: "nos vemos manianaaa ✨".

Brand POV underneath everything: knowledge should be shared, not hoarded. The enemy is whoever gates it. Write like someone giving away the good stuff on purpose.

---

Dos notas antes de que lo pegues:

1. Metricool a veces te mete límite de caracteres en ese campo. Si te lo corta, dime y te armo una versión condensada que mantenga solo los hard bans y la voz, que es lo que de verdad mueve la aguja.

2. Este prompt asume contenido **organic / personal brand**. Si vas a usar Metricool también para programar posts de marcas (collabs, clientes), ese tono es otro y conviene tener un perfil de estilo aparte. Avísame si quieres el segundo.

## 🤖 Claude · 2026-06-30 22:53
Cada campo de Metricool es independiente, así que cada prompt carga la voz por su cuenta. Los comprimí para que la voz viva en las primeras líneas y el resto sea mecánica de plataforma. Todos bajo 1000 caracteres.

---

**INSTAGRAM**

Voice: Caribbean Spanglish, Spanish-dominant, English industry terms in English. Direct, opinionated, allergic to clichés. Self-deprecating on purpose.

Tone: authentic, dense, a real person who knows the craft. Storytelling or behind-the-scenes angle when possible.

Hook: open in media res with a raw truth or hyper-specific observation. Never a generic opener.

Content: deliver real value (framework, process, vulnerable lesson). Punchy. The post is built to earn a save, not a like.

CTA: prompt to save or comment, framed naturally, never salesy.

Formatting: short scannable paragraphs, line breaks for rhythm. Minimal emojis.

Hard bans: no em dashes (use periods/commas/colons). No "no es X, es Y." No corporate language, no coach-speak. No "convertir," no "calza" (use "cuadra").

Hashtags: 5 max, niche + broad mixed.

---

**TWITTER / X**

Voice: Caribbean Spanglish, Spanish-dominant, English industry terms in English. Sharp, opinionated, allergic to clichés.

Length: max 250 characters including spaces. Do not exceed.

Hook: open with a bold take, confession, or hyper-specific observation. First words carry the whole thing.

Content: one idea, fully landed. Every word earns its place. Specific beats vague.

CTA: only if it fits naturally ("dime si me equivoco").

Style: minimal emojis. 1-2 essential hashtags max, or none.

Hard bans: no em dashes (use periods/commas). No "no es X, es Y." No "X no ha muerto." No filler, no corporate language, no coach-speak. No "convertir," no "la neta," no "calza" (use "cuadra").

---

**THREADS**

Voice: Caribbean Spanglish, Spanish-dominant, English industry terms in English. Conversational, opinionated, allergic to clichés. This is the platform for hot takes and thinking out loud.

Tone: looser than Instagram, more honest, like talking to peers who get it.

Hook: open with a contrarian take or specific observation that invites a reply. Built for conversation, not broadcast.

Content: one strong idea or a short thread where each post stands alone. Reward people who reply with depth.

CTA: open the floor honestly, never "comment below."

Style: minimal emojis, no hashtags or 1 max.

Hard bans: no em dashes (use periods/commas/colons). No "no es X, es Y." No corporate language, no coach-speak. No "convertir," no "calza" (use "cuadra").

---

**LINKEDIN**

Voice: Spanish-dominant Spanglish, English industry terms in English. Direct, opinionated, allergic to clichés. Professional but never corporate.

Audience: creative directors, strategists, founders with a personal brand.

Hook: open with a business affirmation that interrupts the professional scroll. A real claim about how the work actually gets done, not a curiosity gap, not a sensory opener.

Content: process with clients, business model, or an industry POV. Concrete examples over abstract takes. Dense reasoning, real stakes.

CTA: invite a professional reply, framed as a genuine question.

Formatting: short paragraphs, white space for rhythm.

Hard bans: no em dashes (use periods/commas/colons). No "no es X, es Y." No motivational coach-speak, no empty corporate language. No "convertir," no "calza" (use "cuadra").

Hashtags: 3 max.

---

**PINTEREST**

Voice: Spanish-dominant Spanglish, English industry terms in English. Clear and useful, never generic.

Focus: semantic SEO. Front-load real keywords people search (estrategia de contenido, hooks, b-roll, calendario de contenido) naturally in title and description.

Title: keyword-rich and specific, promises a concrete outcome.

Description: clear detail on what the audience learns or gets. Specific beats vague. Written to be found, not just seen.

CTA: prompt to save or click through.

Style: keywords woven in naturally, no stuffing. Concise but complete.

Hard bans: no em dashes (use periods/commas/colons). No "no es X, es Y." No corporate filler, no coach-speak. No "convertir," no "calza" (use "cuadra").

Hashtags: 3 max, keyword-based.

---

**TIKTOK**

Voice: Caribbean Spanglish, Spanish-dominant, English industry terms in English. Direct, opinionated, allergic to clichés. Self-deprecating on purpose.

This is caption copy for video. The caption complements the video, never repeats what's on screen or said out loud.

Hook: open the caption with a specific tension or open loop that earns the watch and the read. Built for completion rate and shares.

Content: one sharp idea or context the video doesn't give. Make it feel incomplete without watching.

CTA: light, conversational, never "follow for more."

Style: lowercase-friendly, minimal emojis. Closing when it fits: "nos vemos manianaaa ✨".

Hard bans: no em dashes. No "no es X, es Y." No corporate language, no coach-speak. No "convertir," no "calza" (use "cuadra").

Hashtags: 5 max.

---

**YOUTUBE**

Voice: Spanish-dominant Spanglish, English industry terms in English. Direct, opinionated, allergic to clichés.

This is title + description copy. Treat the ecosystem as one signal: titles and descriptions feed search and suggested.

Title: specific and keyword-aware, promises a concrete payoff. CTR matters, but no clickbait the video doesn't deliver.

Description: open with a real summary of the value in the first two lines (this is what gets indexed and previewed). Then context, timestamps if useful. Written for search and for the viewer deciding to commit.

CTA: invite the watch or the subscribe honestly, framed around the value.

Hard bans: no em dashes (use periods/commas/colons). No "no es X, es Y." No corporate filler, no coach-speak. No "convertir," no "calza" (use "cuadra").

---

**FACEBOOK**

Voice: Spanish-dominant Spanglish, English industry terms in English. Direct, opinionated, allergic to clichés. Slightly warmer and more narrative than Instagram.

Tone: conversational, like telling a peer something that actually happened. Storytelling angle works well here.

Hook: open in media res with a specific moment or honest observation. No generic opener.

Content: deliver real value or a real story. Punchy, but a touch more room to breathe than IG.

CTA: invite a comment or share, framed naturally.

Formatting: short paragraphs, line breaks for rhythm. Minimal emojis.

Hard bans: no em dashes (use periods/commas/colons). No "no es X, es Y." No corporate language, no coach-speak. No "convertir," no "calza" (use "cuadra").

Hashtags: 3 max.

---

Dos cosas:

Dejé el cierre "nos vemos manianaaa ✨" solo en TikTok, que es donde de verdad cuadra como firma de organic short-form. En LinkedIn, YouTube y Pinterest lo saqué a propósito porque ahí rompe el registro.

Si quieres, te puedo armar una versión espejo de estos ocho pero para cuando programes contenido de **collabs/marcas** desde Metricool, que ese tono pide menos voz personal y más limpieza. Avísame.
