## The Audio Goal
The viewer should hear it and think: *"that's exactly what's in my head."*
Not: *"this brand is talking at me."*

---

## Audio Direction Options

**Option 1: The Internal Monologue**
Calm, first-person, almost like thinking out loud.

*"We added a kid. Then I started working from home. Then my parents needed a room. And somewhere in there… the house just stopped working. It's not falling apart. It just — doesn't fit anymore. And every morning I walk through it and think, we really need to fix this."*

**Option 2: The Quiet Realization**
Reflective tone. Like someone processing a moment after a dinner party or a visit to a friend's house.

*"We've lived here seven years. Done nothing to it. And then we went to their place last weekend — and I just… noticed. I noticed everything. Drove home and looked at our kitchen differently. Haven't stopped thinking about it since."*

**Option 3: The Honest Admission**
Slightly frustrated, self-aware. Speaks to the deferred decision.

*"I keep saying we'll get to it. We've been saying that for two years. Meanwhile I'm working from a corner of the dining room, the kitchen doesn't have enough storage, and guests are politely not saying anything. It's time. It's just — finding the right people is the part I keep putting off."*

**Option 4: The Identity Gap**
Calm but pointed. Speaks to the Social Mirror trigger specifically.

*"Everything else in our life reflects where we are right now. The house is the one thing that still looks like ten years ago. That gap has gotten harder to ignore."*

---

## What Makes These Work for Avatar #2

- **No voiceover announcer energy** — it needs to sound like a real person, not an ad
- **Specific friction details** — "corner of the dining room," "not enough storage," "guests not saying anything" — specificity is what creates the *seen* feeling
- **No solution mentioned yet** — at the trigger stage, the audio should land in the problem, not the pitch. Let the visual and hook carry the recognition. The solution comes later.
- **Tone: calm, self-aware, slightly tired of waiting** — that's exactly where this avatar lives at the trigger moment

The closer the audio sounds to a real conversation they've had with their spouse at 10pm, the harder it lands.
