> **Conversation overview**

The person is a content creator working on a paid collaboration with Pippit AI, creating a short-form video script targeting creative people who feel limited by their animation and technical skills. Their core message is that AI doesn't replace creatives but empowers them with resources they always wanted. Their handle is @adrianavmarquez. They came to Claude with three rough structural ideas for the video and worked iteratively through the full script, copy, visual direction, and document production.

From there, the person built out a beat-by-beat visual script including specific shots: a laptop opening reveal, a puppet-style animated photo, a split screen showing the Pippit AI workflow alongside a POV sequence of their eyes at the keyboard, and a hero animation of a can being crushed and reversed. Con Pippit AI y tu imaginación, lo construyes TODO." The person established their full document style preferences: brand colors from their Mini Manual de Marca (#D72323 red, #3846C4 blue, #FFBA35 yellow, #000000, #FFFFFF, #F2E9FF lavender), Poppins font, bold editorial style with color used intentionally and sparingly, black borders on tables, header row text in brand color, body text black on white. After discovering that docx format couldn't reliably embed Poppins, they chose PDF as the output format going forward. They also corrected Claude on voice consistency in the production notes, specifying that notes should be written in infinitive or first-person style rather than second person directed at them. The final PDF was structured across three pages: script table, prompt template with screenshot image, and production notes, each on its own page to avoid content being cut off.
