# Platforms for extracting generational insights

## 🤖 Claude · 2026-05-08 19:00
From Sarah Levinger's research framework, here are the platforms mapped to what you're trying to extract:

**Generational context and worldview** → Pew Research Center, academic generational studies. Look for formative economic events, technology adoption timelines, and trust patterns. This tells you which proof formats and authority signals will land with the generation.

**How your audience talks about their life** → Reddit (age-specific subs), Facebook Groups, Quora, LinkedIn, niche forums. Look for language patterns, complaints, and what they celebrate vs. what frustrates them. Gives you actual customer vocabulary.

**Cultural and economic forces shaping decisions** → Bureau of Labor Statistics, Census data, news archives. Look for cost-of-living shifts, career patterns, and family structure trends. Reveals hidden objections (e.g., financial stress that would make a "treat yourself" angle backfire).

**Real conversation patterns by age group** → YouTube comment sections, podcast reviews, Amazon Q&A. Look for tone, vocabulary, level of skepticism, and the kinds of questions they ask. Tells you whether to lead with proof or lead with story.

A key reminder Sarah hammered: when doing this research, you're not writing down angles. You're building an internal library so you become a "registered expert on the chosen human" for your brand — absorb the worldview first, then create from it later.
