**Key learnings & principles**

**Content and copy:**
- The copywriting quality filter (applied to all content): (1) Can I visualize it? (2) Can I falsify it? (3) Can nobody else say it? Three "no" = discard. Three "yes" = strong.
- Hook has three simultaneous jobs: stop scroll, filter audience, earn the next 5 seconds. Three modes (visual, text, audio) must complement — never repeat — each other.
- Specificity always beats vagueness: real numbers > generic phrases.
- "No es X, es Y" restriction applies ONLY to hooks/opening lines as a literal predictable formula. Contrastive logic inside the body of developed arguments is fine.
- "3 guionados tips" are dead (ChatGPT gives those). The infalible expert loses traction. What wins: documenting process with vulnerability ("document, don't create"), serialized content, strong industry opinions, visual essays.
- Two content pillars that work together: (1) Opinion/Philosophy (drives shares + community), (2) Technical Frameworks (drives saves + reach).
- Hook tactics that dominate in 2026: Confession, Bold Affirmation, Relatability, Contrast, Curiosity/open loop.
- Top performer patterns to reference: @juliabroome (frameworks > tips), @omgadrian (cinematic talking head + vulnerability), @personalbrandlaunch (open loops + high-value carousels).

**Business and negotiation:**
- Never quote rates verbally on calls.
- Production costs are fixed regardless of distribution outcomes — reject CPM/performance models as a default.
- Paid usage, whitelisting, exclusivity, and revisions are quoted separately from base rates.
- Usage rate card: 30 days = 30% of content fee, 60 days = 40%, 90 days = 50%, perpetual = 100%.
- Always require signed contract and cleared deposit before production begins.
- Net-new scope is a paid addition unless Adri explicitly decides otherwise before the partner responds.
- Verify brand contacts via official email domains before advancing any negotiation.

**Contracts and documents:**

- California law is standard for brand-side contracts — not worth contesting on smaller deals.
- Governing law jurisdiction is negotiating leverage, not a dealbreaker on small deals.

**Tax and financial:**
