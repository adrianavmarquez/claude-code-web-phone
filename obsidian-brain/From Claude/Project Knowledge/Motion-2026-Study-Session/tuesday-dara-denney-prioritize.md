## Summary

Evan Lee opens with bootcamp logistics, curriculum recap (Foundations → Sprints → Masters), and community/swag callouts. Dara Denney then delivers her core session on bridging research and execution: she covers how to analyze personas in two distinct ways (who ads target now vs. who actually buys), how to diagnose a brand using a 5-step process, and how to build a creative roadmap split into Creators / Icebox / Quarterly Planning / Monthly Roadmap. Throughout, she demonstrates Claude (specifically Claude co-work) as her primary tool for analyzing Ads Libraries and customer reviews, using Oats Overnight as a running case study. The session closes with Q&A on Claude usage, budgeting, creative testing methodology, and trends-based ideas, plus Evan's homework walkthrough.

## Frameworks / models / taxonomies presented

- **Name**: Two Ways to Think About Personas
- **Definition**: Distinguish between who your ads are currently targeting in market vs. who is actually buying the product. The gap between them is the strategist's opportunity.
- **Structure**:
  1. Who your ads are actually targeting (analyze via Motion Intended Audience or Claude Ads Library analysis)
  2. Who your customers actually are (analyze via customer reviews/CSV through LLM)
- **Introduced at**: 14:27
- **Mode**: both
- **Attribution**: presenter's own

- **Name**: How to Diagnose Your Brand (5-step)
- **Definition**: Process to arrive at a 1–2 sentence diagnosis that becomes the creative strategist's anchor point.
- **Structure**:
  1. Conduct your research
  2. Analyze your personas BOTH ways
  3. Understand your awareness & sophistication levels
  4. Determine your creative diversity level
  5. Arrive at your 1–2 sentence diagnosis
- **Introduced at**: 36:13
- **Mode**: both
- **Attribution**: presenter's own

- **Name**: How to Rank Evidence (8-tier confidence ladder)
- **Definition**: Ranking creative ideas by the strength of evidence supporting them.
- **Structure**:
  - High confidence: (1) Pressure-tested past performance / iterations, (2) New formats for proven messaging, (3) Partnership ads for proven performance & messaging, (4) New insights/hooks from proven personas, (5) New personas from thorough research
  - Low confidence: (6) Working for our competitors, (7) Competitor's longest-running ad, (8) Because our competitors did it
- **Introduced at**: 40:11
- **Mode**: both
- **Attribution**: presenter's own

- **Name**: Golden Rules of Roadmapping
- **Structure**:
  1. Write all your ideas down in a central location
  2. Rank what you have the highest confidence in
  3. Prioritize what is the quickest to get out the door
  4. Map out big swings that have massive upside (personas + big production)
  5. Leave room for creativity and magic, but don't let it distract you
- **Introduced at**: 41:46
- **Mode**: both
- **Attribution**: presenter's own

- **Name**: Creative Roadmap Structure (4 tabs)
- **Structure**:
  1. Creators — list of creators you want to work with
  2. Icebox — central catch-all for all ideas
  3. Quarterly Planning — holidays, launches, persona rollout
  4. Monthly Roadmap Template — persona + 1–2 sentence idea + 3 messaging options per concept
- **Introduced at**: 43:37
- **Mode**: both
- **Attribution**: presenter's own

- **Name**: The Sprint Structure
- **Structure**:
  1. Creative roadmaps built on a monthly cadence
  2. Tasks prioritized and assigned weekly
  3. Daily or bi-daily stand-ups
  4. Creative retro with team or client (loops back to roadmap)
- **Introduced at**: 39:07
- **Mode**: visual
- **Attribution**: presenter's own

- **Name**: 5 Stages of Marketing Awareness (Eugene Schwartz)
- **Structure**: Unaware → Problem Aware → Solution Aware → Product Aware → Most Aware
- **Introduced at**: 29:37
- **Mode**: visual
- **Attribution**: external (classic copywriting framework, applied by Dara)

- **Name**: Creative Tier Classification
- **Structure**: Tier 1 = iteration edits; Tier 2 = post-production edits; Tier 3 = full production / content
- **Introduced at**: 39:07
- **Mode**: spoken
- **Attribution**: presenter's own

## Tactics recommended (do this)

- **Tactic**: Use Motion's Intended Audience feature in AI tagging for a quick gut-check on which audiences your top creatives are actually reaching. — Dara Denney, 15:30
- **Tactic**: Run any brand's Ads Library through Claude (specifically Claude co-work) with a structured prompt covering format breakdown, partnership vs. brand split, core messaging strategies, inferred personas, and a top-10-by-impressions deep dive. — Dara Denney, 16:15
- **Tactic**: Export customer reviews to CSV and prompt the LLM to (1) extract core personas with name/age/1-line description, (2) write a 2–3 sentence emotional narrative per persona, (3) pull 2–3 verbatim quote signals per persona, (4) rank personas by volume AND by emotional intensity, (5) end with a media-buyer-style demographic summary. — Dara Denney, 17:39
- **Tactic**: Always have the LLM cite the verbatim review quotes that justified each persona — to verify the AI's work. — Dara Denney, 19:46
- **Tactic**: Sort competitor Ads Library by impressions high-to-low; the top two rows are typically the best-performing creatives. — Dara Denney, 22:53
- **Tactic**: Map all top-performing creatives onto the 5 awareness levels to see where your funnel is concentrated. — Dara Denney, 29:37
- **Tactic**: Re-diagnose your brand roughly every quarter as new learnings come in. — Dara Denney, 32:00
- **Tactic**: Take winning hooks/text overlays from top video ads and port them onto static ads — often becomes a new top performer. — Dara Denney, 35:55
- **Tactic**: Build out a 4-tab roadmap (Creators / Icebox / Quarterly / Monthly Template) and duplicate the monthly tab each month for agency clients. — Dara Denney, 43:37
- **Tactic**: For each monthly roadmap row, document persona + 1–2 sentence concept + 3 messaging variations (hooks/angles). — Dara Denney, 46:43
- **Tactic**: Pull low-hanging-fruit iterations into the current week's sprint even if they aren't on the quarterly plan. — Dara Denney, 45:55
- **Tactic**: Build a per-client Claude project where you upload context docs, persona research, and past performance so all briefing/hook brainstorming stays localized. — Dara Denney, ~52:00
- **Tactic**: Develop an MVP process for one-off promotional ad requests so you can fulfill them quickly without derailing your evergreen strategy. — Dara Denney, 32:55

## Anti-patterns (don't do this)

- **Anti-pattern**: Don't bring "this competitor has been running this ad for X days" as your only evidence — it's low-confidence; you don't know if it's buried in a retargeting campaign. — Dara Denney, 40:47
- **Anti-pattern**: Don't let the desire for novelty/creativity distract from doubling down on what's already working. — Dara Denney, 42:47
- **Anti-pattern**: Don't accept "make the Mother's Day 25% off static" as your job as a creative strategist — push back on short-shelf-life promotional requests. — Dara Denney, 32:38
- **Anti-pattern**: Don't use AI for the *first pass* of research — Dara prefers human-led initial research, then AI for review-mining and downstream synthesis. — Dara Denney, ~52:30
- **Anti-pattern**: Don't get mad at people using ChatGPT — refusing to develop AI fluency is a survival risk in this next era. — Dara Denney, ~58:30
- **Anti-pattern**: Don't run a creative program without a written roadmap — week-to-week reactivity is what separates struggling teams from clear ones. — Dara Denney, 38:25
