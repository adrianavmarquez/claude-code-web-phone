## Summary

Creative strategists Darcy Tennant (in-house, Heights) and Blaise Ormond (agency, MTRX Media) share frameworks for successfully onboarding into a new creative strategy role. Darcy presents a detailed first-month playbook covering team meetings, tooling, account review via Motion's Runeth AI, making first ads, iterating on winners, and structuring brainstorms. Blaise delivers a "Declassified Creative Strategist Survival Guide" built around the SARA pillars (Speed, Accuracy, Research, Analytics) for agency life. The session closes with extensive Q&A on portfolios, agency-vs-in-house tradeoffs, networking, handling the "managed spend" question, and what makes candidates stand out or get ruled out.

## Live worked examples

- **What was demonstrated**: Creative iteration on a Heights winning ad — taking a podcast-style founder/ambassador testimonial and re-shooting it as an AI-voiced founder reading a tightened script with a pen pointing along handwritten paper.
- **Tools / surfaces shown**: Motion (performance data), AI voice cloning for founder, lo-fi paper-and-pen format
- **Walkthrough steps**:
  1. Identified winning ad via account review (founder × ambassador podcast format, strong CPA/CTR/thumbstop)
  2. Diagnosed the *driver* of performance: the script/spoken content, not the visual format
  3. Borrowed a trending lo-fi format (pen pointing along handwritten script + AI voiceover) seen in another brand's ad
  4. Used AI to clone the founder's voice and tighten the script
  5. Tested — early results showed promising CPA, higher ROAS, retained CTR and thumbstop
- **Outcome**: Validated the "bigger swing iteration" framework — keep what's working (script), radically change format
- **Timestamp**: 18:44

- **What was demonstrated**: Walkthrough of Blaise's actual junior creative strategy portfolio that landed him a job
- **Tools / surfaces shown**: Canva
- **Walkthrough steps**:
  1. Title page: "Creative Strategy Portfolio — Blaise Ormond"
  2. Brand 1 page (Freebird/Freedom Grooming): UGC + HQ Production sections with embedded videos, role labeled (Copywriting + Strategy + Direction)
