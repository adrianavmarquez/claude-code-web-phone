## Summary

This is the kickoff session of the Motion 2026 Creative Strategy Bootcamp. Evan Lee welcomes the cohort, walks through the 8-week curriculum structure (Foundations → Sprints → Masters), bootcamp perks (60-day Motion trial, certification, swag, role opportunities with Hexclad/Jones Road/Ridge), and then delivers the foundational lesson: creative strategy is about identifying the highest-leverage creative for a brand, and the hook is the highest-leverage creative variable. He uses interactive pop quizzes (Ridge Wallet ads, MKBHD format tests, Motion's own bootcamp ads) and a 3-layer hook framework (Visual / Audio + Textual / Psychological) to teach hook analysis, then introduces Week 1 homework: build a hook board in Motion.

## Frameworks / models / taxonomies presented

- **Name**: The 3 Altitudes of a Creative Strategist ("Doer and a Thinker")
- **Definition**: A top-down operating model describing the three levels at which a creative strategist must work.
- **Structure**:
  - **Organization** — What SKU should we focus on? What are the unit economics for each SKU? Who is the intended customer?
  - **Team** — Plan volume and pipeline; prep all teams on goals and execution.
  - **Individual** — Make great ads; write briefs; gather inspo.
- **Introduced at**: 21:52
- **Mode**: visual (progressive reveal)
- **Attribution**: presenter's own

- **Name**: The Hook's 3 Jobs
- **Definition**: The three things a hook must accomplish in performance creative.
- **Structure**:
  1. Generates an emotional reaction (cuts through noise).
  2. Targets and disqualifies (calls out the intended audience).
  3. Naturally connects to the sales story (avoids "hook slop").
- **Introduced at**: 34:00
- **Mode**: visual (fish hook diagram)
- **Attribution**: presenter's own

- **Name**: The 3 Interconnected Creative Layers of a Hook
- **Definition**: The three layers of analysis that explain why a hook works.
- **Structure**:
  1. Visual — Why did this make me stop?
  2. Audio + Textual — How did these enhance the visual hook?
  3. Psychological — What tactics are they using to make you buy?
- **Introduced at**: 35:04
- **Mode**: visual
- **Attribution**: presenter's own

- **Name**: Hook Analytics Funnel
- **Definition**: A sequential metric framework for objectively evaluating whether a hook worked.
- **Structure**:
  1. Spend — Did Meta give it a chance?
  2. Thumbstop — Did it stop people?
  3. Hold Rate — Did they keep watching?
  4. CTR (outbound) — Did they click?
  5. Hard Conversion (ROAS, Purchases, Leads, Sales Calls Booked) — Did they convert?
- **Introduced at**: 49:26
- **Mode**: visual (with example metrics from 3 Motion bootcamp ads)
- **Attribution**: presenter's own

- **Name**: Top 5 Hook Formats (from $1.3B in ad spend)
- **Definition**: The dominant hook formats observed across accounts spending $1M+/month, presented as a companion resource.
- **Structure**: Confession, Bold Claim, Relatability, Contrast, Curiosity.
- **Introduced at**: 73:52
- **Mode**: visual (Hook Tactics Guide reference)
- **Attribution**: Motion (Hook Tactics Guide)

## Tactics recommended (do this)

- **Tactic**: Slow down when starting a new creative strategist role — get to know the business before executing. — Evan Lee, 21:23
- **Tactic**: Operate at three altitudes (Organization → Team → Individual) and ask which altitude the situation requires. — Evan Lee, 21:53
- **Tactic**: Start at the org level by asking leadership about SKU focus, unit economics, and target customer. — Evan Lee, 22:26
- **Tactic**: Analyze every hook through 3 layers (Visual / Audio + Textual / Psychological) and identify which layer "carries" the ad. — Evan Lee, 35:04
- **Tactic**: Take a winning hook and apply it to new body content (hybrid iteration), as Ridge did with the MKBHD ad. — Evan Lee, 11:55
- **Tactic**: Match hook format to audience psychology — like matching bait to fish species (Taimen fish metaphor). — Evan Lee, 32:50
- **Tactic**: Use a sequential metric funnel (Spend → Thumbstop → Hold → CTR → Conversion) to diagnose where a hook succeeded or failed. — Evan Lee, 49:26
- **Tactic**: Set a minimum spend threshold (~$300 at Motion) before judging an ad's performance. — Evan Lee, 56:00
- **Tactic**: Lead with the strongest anchor element — for the Descript ad, move the "remove filler words" payoff to the start instead of waiting until ~12 seconds. — Evan Lee, 47:58
- **Tactic**: For aesthetic/lifestyle products (e.g., phone cases), prioritize fitting and standing out from the target's feed vibe over shock-style hooks. — Evan Lee, 69:00
- **Tactic**: Practice hook analysis every time you doomscroll using the 3 questions. — Evan Lee, 58:33
- **Tactic**: Build a Motion board of at least 3 hooks with notes explaining visual/audio/psychological rationale, then share in Slack. — Evan Lee, 72:29
- **Tactic**: Use Motion's Inspo tab paths (Brand Intel, Trending, Discover, Organic, Chat) to gather hook inspiration. — Evan Lee, 78:43
- **Tactic**: Tag Motion (Creative Analytics) on social with learnings/notes for chances to win swag. — Evan Lee, 18:37

## Anti-patterns (don't do this)

- **Anti-pattern**: Treating creative strategy as endless briefs or filling a content calendar. — Evan Lee, 21:31
- **Anti-pattern**: Making a million ads straight out the gate in a new role. — Evan Lee, 21:58
- **Anti-pattern**: Operating at the individual level when the business requires org-level thinking. — Evan Lee, 24:30
- **Anti-pattern**: "Hook slop" — using a shocking hook (e.g., shooting a gun in the air for a makeup brand) that doesn't connect to the sales story. — Evan Lee, 34:30
- **Anti-pattern**: Copy-pasting trending ad formats without understanding your specific customer. — Evan Lee, 33:32
- **Anti-pattern**: Spying on competitor ad libraries and just ripping off their content. — Ad shown, 29:06
- **Anti-pattern**: Burying the strongest hook element (e.g., Descript waiting ~12s to demonstrate the "ums" feature). — Evan Lee, 47:58
- **Anti-pattern**: Comparing ads with vastly different spend levels (e.g., $5 vs $100K). — Evan Lee, 56:00
- **Anti-pattern**: Throwing spaghetti at the wall with expensive influencer talent — instead, run structured format tests like Ridge did with MKBHD. — Evan Lee, 7:24
