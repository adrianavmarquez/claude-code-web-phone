## Summary

This Week 4 Thursday coaching session focuses on the "OG way" of creating ads — writing scripts, storyboards, and briefs — to complement Tuesday's AI-generation lesson. Alysha Boehm reframes hi-fi vs. lo-fi creative as a spectrum rather than a binary and shares three diagnostic questions for deciding when to invest in polished creative. Eric Philippou shares his static image brief template and a Claude-based automation workflow that has saved him 35 hours/week, and Matteo Friend presents a framework for engineering authenticity in UGC through sourcing, briefing, and creator relationships.

## Frameworks / models / taxonomies presented

- **Hi-fi vs. Lo-fi spectrum** (Alysha) — Reframes the binary into a spectrum with a "middle ground" hybrid. Hi-fi = lighting, set design, professional crew, locked brand colors, looks like an ad. Lo-fi = ugly, shot on phone, unplanned feel, creator-led, doesn't look like an ad. Introduced 00:11:43.
- **Three questions before prioritizing hi-fi** (Alysha) — 1) Are you selling pain or desire? 2) What does your brand need (aspiration/trust/proof)? 3) Define what it means to "win" or "work." Introduced 00:17:54.
- **Creative Strategy Engine** (Alysha) — Pain vs. desire as the primary organizing principle, mapped to audiences. Referenced 00:18:00.
- **Static Image Ad Building Blocks (8 components)** (Eric) — Hero Visual, Headline, Subheadline, Benefits, Pain Points, CTA, Social Proof, Footer/Disclaimer. Many are optional. Introduced 00:34:12.
- **Brief Template — two jobs** (Eric) — Describe the layout, then fill the contents. Introduced 00:35:36.
- **Claude Briefing Automation (3 inputs)** (Eric) — Prompt template + Context File 1 (CSV reference menu) + Context File 2 (visual Canva reference). Introduced 00:37:22.
- **Engineering Authenticity Reframe** (Matteo) — Strategist's job (define, source, brief, share data, build relationship) vs. Creator's job (perform, not strategize). Introduced 00:46:30.
- **Three components of "authentic"** (Matteo) — Shooting style, delivery style, the story. Introduced 00:49:18.
- **Sourcing criteria** (Matteo) — 1) Camera presence (comfort, not polish), 2) Micro-persona match (lived experience). Introduced 00:52:00.
- **Brief structure** (Matteo) — Part A: Brand & product context. Part B: Inspiration, data & what's working. Part C: Script & creative freedom (key points only vs. word-for-word). Introduced 00:54:45.
- **Compounding effect of rehiring** (Matteo) — 1st collab (good) → 3rd (better) → 5th+ (winning) + audience familiarity effect. Introduced 00:58:34.

## Tactics recommended (do this)

- Answer the three diagnostic questions before deciding hi-fi vs. lo-fi investment. — Alysha
- Match creative fidelity to brand goals and funnel position; test both. — Alysha & Eric
- Use a simple Google Sheets content tracker; designers often prefer this over ClickUp/Notion. — Eric
- Use a templated Google Doc brief that describes layout first, then fills contents. — Eric
- Feed Claude three inputs (prompt template, CSV reference menu, visual Canva file) and use Projects to avoid re-uploading. — Eric
- Automate low-impact keyboard work; reinvest saved time into higher-impact thoughtwork (analysis, strategy). — Eric
> *[line redacted — references an out-of-scope venture]*
- If CTR is low, make the CTA clearer. — Eric
- Treat the brief like an interview; record a Loom or jump on a 10-min call with the creator. — Matteo
- Source creators on X, TikTok, Instagram, Saral, JoinBrands, Billo, Breakaway, Insense, Hive House, Backstage. — Matteo & Eric
- Match creator to micro-persona (actual lived experience with the pain point). — Matteo
- Share top-performing videos and pattern data with creators. — Matteo
- Give creators permission to change words while hitting key points; read scripts out loud. — Matteo
- Rehire top-performing creators to compound product knowledge and audience familiarity. — Matteo
- For teleprompter use, rehearse extensively, glance away, use hands. — Matteo

## Anti-patterns (don't do this)

- Don't neglect creative analysis to chase a deliverables quota. — Eric
- Don't automate the thinking, research, or creative strategy. — Eric
- Don't volume-max bad ads — quality first, then scale volume. — Eric
- Don't send creators an AI-written script verbatim, a ChatGPT feature list, or value props with no emotional context. — Matteo
- Don't ask creators to fill strategic gaps — that's the strategist's job. — Matteo
- Don't treat every UGC concept as net-new; iterate on patterns. — Matteo
- Don't hire a creator with no lived experience for a pain-point-driven concept. — Matteo
