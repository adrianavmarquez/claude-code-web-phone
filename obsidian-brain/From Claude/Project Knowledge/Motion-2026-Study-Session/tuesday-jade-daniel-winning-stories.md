## Frameworks / models / taxonomies presented

- **Name**: Explore vs. Exploit
- **Definition**: A two-mode framework for sprint work — Explore is searching for new winners; Exploit is scaling known winners.
- **Structure**:
  - Explore: no winning angle yet; looking for new personas; unsure how to sell a new SKU
  - Exploit: know what works; turn winning angles into new formats; double down on winning personas; push winning SKUs with new creators/formats/offers
- **Introduced at**: 14:19
- **Mode**: visual
- **Attribution**: Motion curriculum (Rory Sutherland popularized the broader concept)

- **Name**: Iteration Tool (2x2)
- **Definition**: A 4-cell test matrix isolating two variables across four ad variants for fast learning.
- **Structure**: Visual variable (V1 vs V2) × Messaging variable (A vs B) → V1A, V1B, V2A, V2B. Example: unboxing vs. talking head × "reasons I love" vs. "reasons I regret."
- **Introduced at**: ~67:06 (during Q&A, Daniel screen-shared)
- **Mode**: visual
- **Attribution**: Daniel Rivera (presenter's own)

- **Name**: Digging for Wells
- **Definition**: Metaphor for creative iteration — when something doesn't work, you're not lost; every test makes the map clearer. Read the ground (data, reviews, creators), then dig again.
- **Structure**: Read the Ground → Find Your Who → Speak Their Language → Find the Emotional Trigger → Stack Learnings (Rinse and Repeat)
- **Introduced at**: 39:21
- **Mode**: visual
- **Attribution**: Daniel Rivera (presenter's own)

- **Name**: How To Read The Ground
- **Definition**: Three input sources for customer insight when ads aren't resonating.
- **Structure**: Persona + Work Data (who's actually buying) → Reviews & Reddit (real customer language) → Creators in the Wild (organic language patterns)
- **Introduced at**: ~44:50
- **Mode**: visual
- **Attribution**: Daniel Rivera

- **Name**: Calm Iteration Process (Idea → $10M+)
- **Definition**: Five-stage process for validating and scaling a new USP.
- **Structure**: Research → Content Partnership → First Tests (3 ads) → Iteration → Scale
- **Introduced at**: ~26:30
- **Mode**: visual
- **Attribution**: Jade Heritage

## Tactics recommended (do this)

- **Tactic**: Combine data insights with qualitative research (organic social comments, customer support, Reddit) to find new angles. — **Speaker**: Jade Heritage — **Context**: Discovering new USPs when current messaging fatigues — **Timestamp**: 22:29
- **Tactic**: Trust your hypotheses and pattern recognition; pitch product changes (new SKUs, features) when you see signal. — **Speaker**: Jade Heritage — **Context**: Strategists driving impact beyond ads — **Timestamp**: ~25:30
- **Tactic**: Start with 2–3 strong concepts at low spend; 5–10 concurrent at higher spend; up to 200/month at scale. — **Speaker**: Jade Heritage — **Context**: Right-sizing test volume to budget — **Timestamp**: ~64:30
- **Tactic**: Use a 2x2 (visual × messaging) iteration tool to isolate variables and learn fast. — **Speaker**: Daniel Rivera — **Context**: Structured A/B testing — **Timestamp**: ~67:06
- **Tactic**: Mine reviews and Reddit threads relentlessly — customers don't perform there, so language is honest. — **Speaker**: Daniel Rivera — **Context**: Reading the ground for new angles — **Timestamp**: ~46:00
- **Tactic**: Pay attention to lower-funnel metrics — sometimes ads work and the offer/checkout is the bottleneck. — **Speaker**: Daniel Rivera — **Context**: Diagnosing whether creative or offer is the issue — **Timestamp**: ~45:00
- **Tactic**: Build a Claude project that pressure-tests copy using reviews and Reddit threads as inputs. — **Speaker**: Daniel Rivera — **Context**: Translating brand truth into customer language — **Timestamp**: 53:35
- **Tactic**: Embrace "lazy" native TikTok-style formats — sometimes simple beats polished. — **Speaker**: Daniel Rivera — **Context**: Speed of learning over production value — **Timestamp**: ~52:00
- **Tactic**: Connect cross-functionally with CX, retail, brand, MMM, and TikTok Shop teams to triangulate insights. — **Speaker**: Daniel Rivera — **Context**: Whose job is audience research — **Timestamp**: ~68:30
- **Tactic**: For small businesses, prioritize ruthlessly — find 2–3 strongest messages, give them longevity, lean on organic signals. — **Speaker**: Jade Heritage — **Context**: Low-budget creative strategy — **Timestamp**: ~72:12
- **Tactic**: Use "expectation gap" copy that creates curiosity (e.g., "smell like $200 for $8"). — **Speaker**: Daniel Rivera — **Context**: Hook construction — **Timestamp**: ~51:30
- **Tactic**: Stack learnings into one ad once you've validated each input independently. — **Speaker**: Daniel Rivera — **Context**: Building undeniable winners — **Timestamp**: ~60:00

## Anti-patterns (don't do this)

- **Anti-pattern**: Don't drill for the customer you assumed — follow the evidence. — **Speaker**: Daniel Rivera — **Context**: Persona discovery — **Timestamp**: 50:40
- **Anti-pattern**: Don't get stuck thinking "we already tried that and it didn't work" — markets and language shift. — **Speaker**: Jade Heritage — **Context**: Openness to re-testing — **Timestamp**: ~23:16
- **Anti-pattern**: Don't spread a small budget across too many concepts — diversify only as you scale. — **Speaker**: Jade Heritage — **Context**: Small-business prioritization — **Timestamp**: ~72:48
- **Anti-pattern**: Don't hammer customers with technical/insider language they don't understand (e.g., "free from parabens, sulfates, dyes" to male body wash buyers). — **Speaker**: Daniel Rivera — **Context**: Customer language alignment — **Timestamp**: ~44:00
- **Anti-pattern**: Don't lean on credibility references your audience doesn't recognize (e.g., "formulated by an ex-Kiehl's chemist" for Midwest male shoppers). — **Speaker**: Daniel Rivera — **Context**: Calibrating proof to audience — **Timestamp**: ~43:30
- **Anti-pattern**: Don't blame creative when lower-funnel metrics (offer, shipping, checkout) are the actual drop-off. — **Speaker**: Daniel Rivera — **Context**: Funnel diagnosis — **Timestamp**: ~45:00
- **Anti-pattern**: Don't over-engineer your data model early — keep attribution clean and simple at low scale. — **Speaker**: Jade Heritage — **Context**: Small-business setup — **Timestamp**: ~73:30
