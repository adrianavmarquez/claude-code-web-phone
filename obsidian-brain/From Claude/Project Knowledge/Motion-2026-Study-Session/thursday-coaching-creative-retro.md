## Frameworks / models / taxonomies presented

- **Name**: How to find personas with no customer reviews
- **Definition**: Four-step workaround for sourcing persona insights when a brand has no first-party reviews.
- **Structure**:
  1. Research your own differentiator first
  2. Direct competitors — scrape their reviews
  3. Amazon comparables — scrape reviews from generic versions of your product
  4. Adjacent brands — scrape reviews from products your ICP also buys
- **Introduced at**: 1:11:18
- **Mode**: visual + live worked example
- **Attribution**: Alysha from Motion (presenter's own)

- **Name**: Monthly Creative Retro (Spacegoods version)
- **Definition**: A structured monthly retrospective combining quantitative ad performance, customer insights, and collaborative brainstorming.
- **Structure**:
  - Top 10 ads by spend with soft metrics (hold rate, CTR, thumb stop, convert score)
  - Creative insights via naming conventions + Motion AI tags (target personas, niche customers, AI-tagged audiences, core benefits)
  - Customer insights (rotated monthly — e.g., purchase timing, brand positioning, motivators/barriers via Claude)
  - Ad spend analysis (top spenders vs. top up-and-comers launched that month)
  - Brainstorming canvas with two core pillars for next month + formats to iterate
- **Introduced at**: 28:49
- **Mode**: live Miro walkthrough
- **Attribution**: Naomi Peh Haeger (presenter's own)

- **Name**: Quarterly Plan Guide (Pillars → Budget → Bandwidth → Diversity)
- **Definition**: Framework for translating account diagnosis into an executable quarterly testing plan.
- **Structure**:
  - Step 1: Pick 3–5 pillars (first pass) based on diagnosis
  - Step 2: Reality-check budget — testing vs. scaling split (default ~70/30, exploration ~80/20, promo ~50/50, big promo up to 90% sale ads)
  - Step 3: Reality-check bandwidth — trim pillars to what production capacity supports
  - Step 4: Plan creative diversity per pillar — minimum 3–4 distinct concepts across formats and funnel stages
- **Introduced at**: 48:45
- **Mode**: Google Doc walkthrough
- **Attribution**: Joanna Wallace (presenter's own)

- **Name**: 5 Ways to Gut Check AI Personas
- **Definition**: Five validation checks to apply to any AI-generated persona output.
- **Structure**:
  1. Ask for receipts (real quotes from source)
  2. Check sample size (statistical significance of underlying data)
  3. Check a second AI (paste raw material into ChatGPT/Gemini, compare)
  4. Check for a trigger (the moment that pushed the persona to seek a solution)
  5. Check for SKU blending (ensure AI is not averaging across products)
- **Introduced at**: 1:25:25
- **Mode**: PDF walkthrough with copy-paste prompts
- **Attribution**: Joanna Wallace (presenter's own)

- **Name**: Pillar / Concept / Angle taxonomy
- **Definition**: Hierarchy of creative strategy terms.
- **Structure**:
  - Pillar = big bucket / learning objective (e.g., "young professionals" persona)
  - Concept = medium execution (e.g., UGC with a young professional)
  - Angle = entry point into the sales sequence (e.g., recommendation, expert, negative framing)
- **Introduced at**: 1:48:41 (in answer to student question)
- **Mode**: verbal
- **Attribution**: Joanna Wallace

## Tactics recommended (do this)

- Include a 1–2 sentence diagnosis (the "context" line) in every creative brief explaining why the ad is being made. — Alysha — 08:06
- Use Joanna's Creative Strategy One-Pager to work through your diagnosis. — Alysha — 08:19
- Before automating any AI workflow, do it manually first to build the gut instinct needed to spot AI errors. — Alysha — ~1:34:00
- Run a creative retro on a monthly cadence (not weekly) with prep distributed 3 days in advance to the team. — Naomi — 28:49 / 47:00
- Keep your Miro board open as a living document; add to it daily, formalize monthly. — Alysha — 47:30
- Use sprints (e.g., one in-house shoot per month or every other month) instead of weekly content churn. — Naomi — 48:03
- Pull top 10 ads by spend (spend is king) when starting a retro. — Naomi — 30:00
- Combine manual naming-convention tags with Motion AI tags to cross-reference targeting (e.g., filter by your "functional optimizers" tag, then look at AI messaging-angle distribution). — Naomi — 1:43:00
- Rotate the customer-insights section of your retro monthly (purchase timing one month, brand positioning another) to keep it fresh and spark new conversations. — Naomi — 41:30
- Default budget split ~70/30 testing vs. scaling; shift to ~80/20 for new/stale accounts and ~50/50 (up to 90% sale ads) during promos. — Joanna — 51:56
- Hedge bets when testing wild ideas: run a "Dennis Rodman" V1 (out-of-the-box) alongside a "boring Joanna" V2 (best practices). — Joanna — 1:25:59
- Plan quarterly with the next quarter's tentpoles in mind (e.g., prep July 4th assets in June). — Joanna — 56:48
- Each pillar needs minimum 3–4 distinct concepts across formats and funnel stages for a fair test. — Joanna — ~58:00
- Build roadmaps per product (especially for hero SKUs), not per persona; pillars sit beneath products. — Joanna — 1:12:24
- For B2B with no reviews: query sales/CS/onboarding calls (Gong, HubSpot), LinkedIn DMs, Slack communities, Facebook groups, blogs. — Alysha — 1:12:00
- For new D2C brands: scrape direct-competitor reviews, Amazon comparables, and adjacent-brand reviews; analyze through Claude with a brand-foundation document. — Alysha — 1:12:27
- When gut-checking AI: ask for receipts, check sample size, run output through a second AI, look for trigger moments, watch for SKU blending. — Joanna — 1:26:41
- When testing hooks, change the entry point (recommendation vs. expert vs. negative vs. personal experience) — not just the headline copy. — Joanna — ~22:00
- Match script to format (UGC vs. voiceover vs. unboxing vs. testimonial); don't reuse the same script across formats. — Alysha — ~17:30
- Use Motion's "Intended Audience" AI tag and Comparative reports to identify gaps between who you think you're speaking to and who Meta is actually serving. — Alysha — 1:35:42

## Anti-patterns (don't do this)

- Testing an idea without a 1–2 sentence diagnosis or strategic reason. — Alysha — 08:15
- Running an intensive creative retro weekly or bi-weekly. — Joanna — 1:45:38
- Scaling the "best of a bad bunch" in an underperforming account. — Joanna — ~54:00
- Sending the same script to multiple creators and calling it a creative test. — Joanna — ~14:00
- Trusting AI persona output without demanding receipts. — Joanna — 1:26:21
- Making creative decisions based on personal taste as a marketer. — Alysha — 1:28:17
- Copying competitor ads directly because they ran them. — Alysha — ~25:30
- Making ads for every SKU instead of focusing on hero products. — Joanna — 1:12:24
- Forgetting to plan tentpole sale assets, then realizing you've over-committed evergreen production. — Joanna — ~50:00
- Testing five pillars when bandwidth supports only two — leads to "hunches and vibes" instead of learnings. — Joanna — ~58:30
- Just swapping the headline on a winning image ad and calling it an iteration (under Andromeda this is too similar). — Joanna / Alysha — ~22:30
- Trying to scale a winning persona without ever testing new audiences in parallel — you'll get caught with your pants down when fatigue hits. — Joanna — 1:02:41

## Student questions answered

- **Q (Patrick Unrath)**: How do you test now under Andromeda — are headline swaps, same-script-different-creator, same-script-different-hook still valid?
  - Answered by: Joanna, Naomi, Alysha — 11:18
  - Summary: No fixed Bible exists; you must test in your own account. Same script with different creators is generally too similar — but visually distinct first-3-second hooks (POV vs. talking head, split-screen vs. single-shot) into the same body script can still work. Match script to format. Change the *entry point/angle*, not just the headline.

- **Q (Hassan Habib)**: Does evidence include competitor data? Is there a strict rule against testing ideas without evidence?
  - Answered by: Alysha, Joanna — 24:16
  - Summary: No strict rule — gut instincts are okay if budget allows. Evidence includes both your own data and competitor data, but use competitor data to identify *patterns and testing mechanisms*, not to copy ads. Always hedge: a wild V1 alongside a best-practice V2.

- **Q (chat)**: What's the difference between a pillar, a concept, and an angle?
  - Answered by: Joanna — 1:48:41
  - Summary: Pillar = big bucket (persona/format/moment). Concept = medium execution (e.g., a UGC with a specific persona). Angle = the entry point into the sales sequence (recommendation, expert, negative, personal experience).

- **Q (Tina, chat)**: What is a "big idea"?
  - Answered by: Joanna, Alysha — 1:49:45
  - Summary: Not a formally defined term in their taxonomy — they use pillar/concept/angle/format. Glossary needs expanding.

- **Q (chat)**: Are you building a roadmap per product or per persona?
  - Answered by: Joanna — ~1:12:00
  - Summary: Per product. Pillars (including personas) sit beneath each product. Don't make ads for every SKU — focus on hero products.

- **Q (Larissa, chat)**: With limited budget and one persona working, should we still set aside budget for new personas?
  - Answered by: Joanna — 1:02:00
  - Summary: Yes — always allocate something for testing new audiences, but be extremely picky and prioritize the highest-potential, largest persona. Use Dara's gap analysis to choose. Stay one step ahead of fatigue.

- **Q (chat, on Naomi's retro)**: How do you know people are seeing ads at night vs. elsewhere? How long does the retro take?
  - Answered by: Naomi — 41:00
  - Summary: Shopify purchase-time data is a hypothesis (not a clean attribution). Retro takes roughly a day, distributed across several days, and is sent to the team 3 days before the live brainstorm.

- **Q (chat)**: Can pillars be repeated quarter to quarter?
  - Answered by: Joanna — ~1:08:00
  - Summary: Yes — once a pillar is proven it joins the scaling repertoire and gets repeated seasonally. Personas evolve year over year, so retest periodically.
