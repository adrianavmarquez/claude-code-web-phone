## Summary

This special edition Week 5 Thursday coaching session focuses entirely on Motion's AI agent, Runneth, and how the top 1% of creative strategists are using it across the creative strategy flywheel. Jose Garcia, Rabia Sohail, and Josh Bampton walk through seven real-world Runneth prompts spanning analytics, research, ideation, briefing, and QA—including analyzing top performers to plan next ads, persona expansion, decoding confusing metrics, competitor pattern/gap matrices, hook-stacked VSL building, and pre-launch ad QA. The session closes with a sneak peek of Runneth 1.0, which integrates with Notion, Google Drive, and other tools to automate brief generation and QA workflows.

## Frameworks / models / taxonomies presented

- **Name**: Creative Strategy System (three core questions)
  - **Definition**: The three foundational questions every creative strategist needs to answer daily.
  - **Structure**: What's working for me? / What's working for others? / What to do next?
  - **Introduced at**: 06:54
  - **Mode**: visual
  - **Attribution**: presenter's own (Jose Garcia / Motion)

- **Name**: Creative Strategy Flywheel
  - **Definition**: A five-stage workflow that defines the creative strategist's process; each stage can be enabled by Runneth.
  - **Structure**: Analytics → Research → Ideation → Briefing → QA
  - **Introduced at**: 11:22
  - **Mode**: visual
  - **Attribution**: presenter's own (Motion)

- **Name**: Hook Stacking (for VSLs)
  - **Definition**: A long-form video script structure that layers hooks across awareness levels to move cold audiences through the funnel in one video.
  - **Structure**: Unaware Hook → Problem-Aware Hook → Solution-Aware Hook → Product-Aware Hook → Winning ad body → Full VSL
  - **Introduced at**: 53:59
  - **Mode**: visual
  - **Attribution**: presenter's own (Jose Garcia)

- **Name**: Three-Layer Content System (Strategic Framework from Runneth output)
  - **Definition**: A launch framework for new scripts segmented across funnel layers, generated by Runneth's analysis.
  - **Structure**: Layer 1 / Layer 2 / Layer 3 (top-of-funnel → bottom-of-funnel)
  - **Introduced at**: 19:30
  - **Mode**: visual
  - **Attribution**: Runneth-generated (anchored on Motion data)

## Tactics recommended (do this)
- **Tactic**: Use Runneth to analyze the last 30 days of performance and generate a 20-script plan for the next 30 days. — Josh Bampton, account-wide analytics + ideation, 13:34
- **Tactic**: Continue the Runneth conversation with follow-up prompts (e.g., "add launch strategy / funnel layers / format mix") to extract more strategic depth. — Josh Bampton, prompt iteration, 18:30
- **Tactic**: When you find a winning persona, prioritize creative diversity within that persona over chasing new ones. — Josh Bampton, persona strategy, 23:20
- **Tactic**: Always ask Runneth to provide rationale alongside its recommendations so you can validate the logic. — Josh Bampton, AI prompting, 22:13
- **Tactic**: For confusing-metric ads, click into the creative detail page and use the Runneth chat scoped to that single asset. — Josh Bampton, micro analysis, 33:00
- **Tactic**: Use Runneth + Inspo to analyze 3–4 competitor brands and build a patterns/gaps matrix when onboarding a new brand with no ad history. — Josh Bampton, research, 41:00
- **Tactic**: Take your top-spending (or top-ROAS, top-hook) ad and ask Runneth for 5 persona-targeted iterations, then promote one to a full brief. — Josh Bampton, scaling winners, 51:18
- **Tactic**: For B2B/SaaS/lead gen brands, build hook-stacked VSLs that walk cold audiences from unaware → product-aware in a single video. — Jose Garcia, long-form content, 53:59
- **Tactic**: Use Runneth as a pre-launch QA reviewer; have it flag issues and generate editor revision briefs against your account's top-performer patterns. — Jose Garcia, QA workflow, 63:53
- **Tactic**: For Andromeda-friendly diversity, vary the visual format dramatically while holding messaging angle constant. — Josh Bampton, creative diversity, 38:35
- **Tactic**: Use Motion's visual format AI tag and Inspo to checklist visual format diversity once you've found a winning message. — Jose Garcia, diversity, 39:30

## Anti-patterns (don't do this)
- **Anti-pattern**: Running product-demo ads without a presenter (didn't drive conversions for Motion). — Josh Bampton, 15:13
- **Anti-pattern**: Using identical messaging across different personas — pivot the comms when changing audience. — Josh Bampton, 25:07
- **Anti-pattern**: Visual diversity that's actually superficial (e.g., same POV/format with cosmetic swaps) — Andromeda will not see this as diverse. — Josh Bampton, 38:00
- **Anti-pattern**: Skit-format ads burned five-figure spend with zero event registrations for Motion's testing campaign. — Josh Bampton, 46:48
- **Anti-pattern**: Brand-story ads without viewer-relevance for conversion campaigns (e.g., OmniPet's Dragon's Den newspaper-style ad). — Josh Bampton, 35:57
- **Anti-pattern**: Hooks built on insider language for cold audiences — they filter out everyone unfamiliar with the jargon. — Josh Bampton, 34:30
- **Anti-pattern**: Managing creative briefs in spreadsheets. — Jose Garcia, 60:53
- **Anti-pattern**: Evaluating ads after fewer than 7 days of testing. — Jose Garcia, 48:34

- **Question**: How would we use Runneth to create net-new concepts and generate scripts?
- **Asked by**: relayed by Rabia Sohail
- **Timestamp**: 20:37
- **Answered by**: Josh Bampton (deferred and demonstrated in subsequent persona example)
- **Answer summary**: Use the persona-expansion prompt; Runneth analyzes current targeting, suggests new personas with rationale and messaging territory, then generates "Starter Concepts" for each new persona that can be promoted to full briefs.

- **Question**: Are we still using detailed targeting for demographics/interests, or is that irrelevant now?
- **Asked by**: relayed by Rabia Sohail
- **Timestamp**: 27:35
- **Answered by**: Josh Bampton & Jose Garcia
- **Answer summary**: Meta is phasing out detailed targeting under Andromeda; creative is now the primary targeting lever. Use Meta's post-launch age/gender breakdowns as a confirmation signal, but invest in messaging-level audience targeting and visual diversity.
- **Topic tags**: targeting, andromeda, creative-diversity

- **Question**: AI can give rationale to almost any argument — how do you know Runneth is telling the truth?
- **Asked by**: Alex
- **Timestamp**: 29:20
- **Answered by**: Jose Garcia
- **Answer summary**: Runneth's outputs are anchored to Motion's structured data (AI tags, performance metrics, account-specific creative). You can always ask Runneth to explain its rationale (e.g., "why is this the lowest-hanging fruit?") and validate against your own data. The role of the strategist is to apply taste on top of data-grounded outputs.
- **Topic tags**: ai-trust, data-driven, creative-taste

- **Question**: Do we always have to wait 30 days to evaluate a test?
- **Asked by**: Monica
- **Timestamp**: 48:28
- **Answered by**: Jose Garcia
- **Answer summary**: No — 7 days minimum, with 7/14/30 as standard windows. Spend thresholds matter more than time alone (~$50 for small budgets, ~$200 typical, ~$1k for $1M+ accounts). Setup depends on media-buyer ad-set structure ensuring each creative gets sufficient spend.
- **Topic tags**: testing, ad-performance, spend-thresholds

- **Question**: When optimizing for Andromeda, how do you make ads differentiated enough to be detected as unique?
- **Asked by**: Patrick
- **Timestamp**: 36:28
- **Answered by**: Josh Bampton & Jose Garcia
- **Answer summary**: Hold messaging angle constant but vary the visual format dramatically (e.g., POV dog vs. cinematic restaurant B-roll for the same vegetarian-dog message). Use Motion's visual format AI tag and Inspo to ensure visual diversity. Stage matters: if you've found a winning message, prioritize visual diversity; if still discovering, vary messaging.
- **Topic tags**: andromeda, creative-diversity, visual-format

- **Question**: How do we know Runneth won't spiral into AI paralysis, telling us to keep changing things forever?
- **Asked by**: Stephen & Alex
- **Timestamp**: 68:47
- **Answered by**: Jose Garcia
- **Answer summary**: Runneth 1.0's QA workflow is being trained to learn from each strategist's reviews — building success criteria from the user's own QA decisions, flagging only ambiguous cases, and approving the rest automatically. Human strategist remains the source of truth.
- **Topic tags**: ai-learning, qa, workflow

- **Question**: With AI generating unlimited ideas, what makes a good creative strategist?
- **Asked by**: relayed by Rabia Sohail
- **Timestamp**: 62:07
- **Answered by**: Jose Garcia & Josh Bampton
- **Answer summary**: Taste — the ability to judge what's good vs. bad creative based on data, customer understanding, and strategic judgment. The strategist's role shifts to directing the AI, validating outputs, and applying brand-specific context that AI lacks.
- **Topic tags**: role-of-strategist, ai-collaboration, creative-taste
