## Summary

Evan Lee delivers a comprehensive guide to creative analytics, structured across three levels: individual ad analysis, campaign/product-level analysis, and macro (weekly/quarterly) review. He demonstrates how to use core metrics (Thumbstop, Hold Rate, CTR outbound, conversion metrics, cost-per-conversion) to diagnose where ads succeed or fail across the funnel — capture attention, keep attention, drive click, and convert/scale — and ties creative performance to landing page diagnosis. The session includes live demonstrations of Motion's reporting views and AI tooling (Runneth and the newly launched Motion Creative Plugin / MCP for Claude) to streamline analysis, hypothesis tracking, and creative briefing.

## Frameworks / models / taxonomies presented

- **Every ad is a hypothesis** (presenter's own, slide 33) — Three scales of hypothesis: **Individual** (hook, CTA, model), **Team-level** (creative volume per product, creators vs internal, statics vs video), **Organization-level** (product focus shifts, success criteria adjustments).
- **Hypothesis Validation Framework** (presenter's own, slide 34) — Three outcomes: **Winner** (validated true → double down), **Loser** (validated false → don't iterate, avoid similar swings), **Middling** (inconclusive → need more time, isolate variables).
- **3 Levels of Creative Analysis** (presenter's own, slide 35) — Individual ad analysis → Campaign/product-level analysis → Macro analysis (90-day reviews).
- **Creative Metric Cheatsheet** (presenter's own, slide 36) — Thumbstop, Hold Rate, CTR (outbound), Conversion Metric, Cost per Conversion Metric — each paired with definition and "evidence it gives you." Augmented by Motion AI Tags: Visual format, Intended Audience, Messaging theme, Angle.
- **Diagnostic Cheatsheet** (presenter's own, slide 37) — Four funnel-stage diagnoses: **Capture attention** (hook flopped → low Thumbstop), **Keep attention** (hook works/body loses → high Thumbstop, low thruplay), **Drive click** (watching but not clicking → CTA/persona mismatch), **Convert and scale** (clicked but didn't buy → landing page misalignment).

## Tactics recommended (do this)

- Anchor analysis to a hypothesis before opening the data.
- Use ~3× CPA or ~3× AOV as a statistically relevant spend threshold before judging a creative. (in response to Ashley's question)
- When Thumbstop is high but CTR is low, suspect clickbait/hook-slop or product mismatch — not always a CTA problem.
- When watch-time is high but CTR is low, strengthen and surface the CTA earlier.
- When watch-time is low but CTR is healthy, shorten the video and bring CTAs higher.
- When CTR is strong but conversions lag, investigate landing page alignment to ad messaging.
- Group ads by product, SKU, creator, hook tactic, or messaging theme to build higher-level hypotheses for resource allocation.
- Track active hypotheses in a log: testing → validated/invalidated → retire false hypotheses.
- Use Runneth or the MCP plugin to generate weekly creative reviews, ad-level comparisons, and briefs.
- For one-person teams: prioritize lowest-hanging fruit (e.g., turn one customer review into one ad in Canva) over building the full system at once.
- If an organic post takes off, test it as a paid ad.
- Share Motion reports directly with creative teams as briefing artifacts to avoid spreadsheet handoffs.

## Anti-patterns (don't do this)

- Being a passive consumer of dashboards — just reporting numbers without forming an independent analytical perspective.
- Getting lost in individual ad weeds without stepping up to product/campaign/macro views.
- Assuming high watch-time + low CTR is always a CTA problem (could be length, value delivery, or persona mismatch).
- Trying to do the full creative-strategy stack as a one-person team without prioritizing.
- Driving high-engagement ads to a generic homepage instead of an aligned landing page.
- Treating creative strategy as ads-only and ignoring organic, landing pages, and offers.
- Calling Meta's API directly at high volume (risk of being banned) — use Motion's aggregated data instead.
