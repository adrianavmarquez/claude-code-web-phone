## Frameworks / models / taxonomies presented

- **Name**: Sarah's Hook Workflow Template
- **Definition**: A visual workflow for iterating on a winning concept to generate new hooks.
- **Structure**:
  1. Concept (e.g., "This is what happened when")
  2. Messaging Angle (Pain Point, Motivation)
  3. Micropersona (e.g., The Everyday Hero / Service Professional)
  4. Hook
  5. Research (Reddit threads, reviews)
- **Introduced at**: 21:51
- **Mode**: visual
- **Attribution**: presenter's own

- **Name**: How Jake writes hooks
- **Definition**: A structured process for developing hooks from high-level strategy down to specific components.
- **Structure**:
    - Strategy: Micro-persona → Motivator → Benefit
    - Format (street interview, podcast, staged moment, etc.)
    - The Hook: Visual action + Voiceover + Header caption
- **Introduced at**: 32:04
- **Mode**: visual
- **Attribution**: presenter's own

- **Name**: Good on-ramp vs. Bad on-ramp
- **Definition**: The on-ramp is the introduction to a video that connects the hook to the sales sequence. A good on-ramp logically extends the hook's premise; a bad one pivots to disconnected content.
- **Introduced at**: 50:15
- **Mode**: visual
- **Attribution**: Joanna Wallace

- **Name**: Hook component trio
- **Definition**: Hooks consist of three components that should work together, not duplicate each other: visual action, voiceover, and header caption.
- **Introduced at**: 32:04
- **Mode**: verbal
- **Attribution**: Jake Abrams

## Tactics recommended (do this)

- **Tactic**: Gamify the opening with a timer or countdown to compel viewers to watch for the payoff.
- **Speaker**: Joanna Wallace
- **Context**: Discussing why the 50% hook rate version of the Eskin shower ad worked.
- **Timestamp**: 47:10

- **Tactic**: Use a real human voiceover (not AI) for testimonial-style ads to retain inflection and emotion.
- **Speaker**: Joanna Wallace
- **Context**: HexClad ad analysis — used an employee's real VO.
- **Timestamp**: 14:11

- **Tactic**: Break up headline copy with caps, bold, italics, or emojis for emphasis.
- **Speaker**: Joanna Wallace
- **Context**: "NEVER" capped in HexClad hook.
- **Timestamp**: 13:03

- **Tactic**: Feed AI your context library (skill docs defining your hook taxonomy) before asking it to write hooks.
- **Speaker**: Jake Abrams
- **Context**: Demonstrated in Runneth walkthrough.
- **Timestamp**: 32:27

- **Tactic**: Show the problem visually rather than only describing it.
- **Speaker**: Sarah Burnett / Alysha Boehm
- **Context**: Car cleaning hook — finger swipe on dashboard.
- **Timestamp**: 10:18

- **Tactic**: Order metrics in your Motion table as first-frame retention → thumb stop rate → hold rate → CTR to track viewer progression.
- **Speaker**: Alysha Boehm
- **Context**: Aim for 90%+ first-frame retention, 30%+ thumb stop, 20%+ hold rate.
- **Timestamp**: ~1:00:00

- **Tactic**: Split heavy copy across multiple clips rather than overwhelming the first frame.
- **Speaker**: Joanna Wallace
- **Context**: HexClad two-clip hook structure.
- **Timestamp**: 14:30

- **Tactic**: When testing a new hook on existing footage, ensure the on-ramp still logically connects to the new opener.
- **Speaker**: Joanna Wallace
- **Context**: Dr. Squatch good vs. bad on-ramp comparison.
- **Timestamp**: 50:57

- **Tactic**: Use the Organic section in Motion or tools like Apify to scrape viral content for research before writing hooks.
- **Speaker**: Jake Abrams
- **Context**: Insurify hook generation example.
- **Timestamp**: 43:25

## Anti-patterns (don't do this)

- **Anti-pattern**: AI voiceover that doesn't match on-screen talent's mouth movements.
- **Speaker**: Sarah Burnett
- **Context**: Eskin shower ad version 2 (28% hook rate).
- **Timestamp**: 46:50

- **Anti-pattern**: Slapping a top-performing hook onto an unrelated sequence (e.g., pit stain hook on a scent variety sequence).
- **Speaker**: Joanna Wallace
- **Context**: Dr. Squatch bad on-ramp example.
- **Timestamp**: 54:45

- **Anti-pattern**: Comment-reply hooks where the "comment" feels staged or perfectly set up to be an ad.
- **Speaker**: Alysha Boehm
- **Context**: Eskin v2 money-back guarantee comment.
- **Timestamp**: 49:27

- **Anti-pattern**: Repeating the product name in the hook before introducing the problem or solution it addresses.
- **Speaker**: Joanna Wallace
- **Context**: Afina shower head hook critique.
- **Timestamp**: ~1:05:30

- **Anti-pattern**: Broad, vague hooks that don't specify who the audience is or what problem is being solved.
- **Speaker**: Sarah Burnett / Jake Abrams / Alysha Boehm
- **Context**: Trinity Ishikawa's content planning hook.
- **Timestamp**: 20:18

- **Anti-pattern**: Using the same copy for header caption and voiceover (redundancy).
- **Speaker**: Jake Abrams
- **Context**: Hook component trio framework.
- **Timestamp**: ~32:00

- **Anti-pattern**: Getting too formulaic with sequence frameworks (problem-agitate-solve) — viewers recognize the formula.
- **Speaker**: Joanna Wallace
- **Context**: Discussion of sales sequences.
- **Timestamp**: 1:03:42

## Live worked examples

- **What was demonstrated**: End-to-end AI-assisted hook generation workflow in Motion's Runneth.
- **Tools/surfaces shown**: Runneth (Motion), with referenced PDFs (vo-hooks-database, visual-action-hooks) and a scraped TikTok CSV.
- **Walkthrough steps**:
  1. Upload hook taxonomy "skill" docs to give AI the user's vocabulary.
  2. Upload TikTok scrape (car insurance niche) for real research.
  3. Prompt AI to deep-dive a single micro-persona ("Budget Savers").
  4. Prompt AI to identify the single strongest motivator ("you're getting ripped off").
  5. Generate hooks for three formats (street interview, podcast, staged moment) with caption/VO/visual action breakdown.
  6. Pivot the same engine for funnel stage (TOF→BOF) and new persona (Parents of Young Drivers).
  7. Consolidate everything into a master table organized by persona, funnel stage, motivator, benefit, format.
- **Outcome**: Comprehensive hook table generated in ~30 minutes that Jake claims would represent ~3 months of manual hook roadmapping.
- **Brand context**: Insurify (auto insurance marketplace).
- **Timestamp**: 32:04–45:46
